A minimum-trust cryptocurrency exchange

This is currently a proof-of-concept of how decentralised a cryptocurrency exchange can be without smart contracts. It is intended to let you trade NANO and BANANO with others while putting as little trust as possible in a third-party, i.e. us.

Please do not trade amounts you wouldn't be comfortable losing (at least, not yet), this is first and foremost an experiment.

Wait, why do I have to send you money to log in?

Very good question. Take a seat.

First of all, we will immediately send any amount you sent to those addresses right back to you.

Secondly, we identify you by your wallet address. You do not need a username or password for this site, and you don't need to give us your e-mail address. You are your wallet.

The amount we ask you to send is unique to your browser session, so when we receive it, we'll know that you are the owner of that wallet address, and we can log you in.

We then automatically send the money back to the address you sent it from, because it's now served its purpose.

Why should I trust you?

You shouldn't. That's the point of decentralisation. To remove the need for a trusted third party. However, because NANO doesn't have smart contracts, an exchange can't be completely decentralised. Instead, we're calling this a "minimum-trust" exchange – we ask for the absolute minimum amount of trust that is needed to make the whole thing work.

Also, we're only asking for fractions of a cent, so if we turn out to be swindlers and run away with your money, you won't be losing much.
